3.- Introduction and pipeline

To fulfil the projectâ€™s aim a pipeline was devised based on the course Analysis of single cell RNA-seq data written by the authors of some the packages used throughout the project (SC3 and scater).

In Figure 3 a schematic representation of the pipeline is shown, with a level for each step performed during the analysis and the 2 main stages (Pre-processing and Visualisation/Clustering) outlined on the background. Below the main steps are listed the order they were performed:

    1. Quality control and filtering to eliminate outliers and reduce the dimensions of the expression matrix by discarding both cells and transcript IDs.
    2. Normalisation and denoising using ERCC spike-ins.
    3. Dimensionality reduction via Principal Component Analysis (PCA), t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE) and Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection (UMAP).
    4. Unsupervised clustering using k-means in the above dimensions and within the SC3 package for R (Kiselev et al., 2017).
    5. Biological analysis of genes found to be differentially expressed in the clusters

It is important to note that several methodologies where assessed in some of the steps outlined above and their comparisons can be found on their respective sections.

Figure 3: Analysis pipeline. Consisting of a stage for pre-processing and another encompassing visualisation and clustering, the different steps performed in this analysis are showed from top (starting with the initial data) to bottom (ending with the biological analysis). scran, R package; CPM, Counts Per Million; PCA, Principal Component Analysis; tSNE, t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbour Embedding; UMAP, Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection; SC3, R package; DE, Differential expression.
